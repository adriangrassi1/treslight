THE STORY
A night train
to Bremen.

I wasn't looking for a van. I was looking for a way to keep moving — and then, late one September night in 2025, I found it on a German website. A 2012 Volkswagen California T5, full camper setup, 4x4, 105,000 km.

The kind of listing that disappears overnight. So I didn't wait. I booked a night train to Bremen, in northern Germany, and by the next morning the California was mine.

"It was a few days before my birthday. The best gift I ever gave myself."

A few weeks later I pointed it south. France first, then Spain, then Portugal. No fixed itinerary — just a direction and a tank of diesel.

LIFE INSIDE
Everything you need.
Nothing you don't.

The California has everything — a bed, a kitchen, a roof that opens to the stars. You cook where you park. You wake up where you fell asleep looking at the horizon.

Van life isn't about adventure every day. It's about the freedom to choose your view for the night. Sometimes it's a mountain. Sometimes it's a beach. Sometimes it's a supermarket car park — and that's fine too.

"The van doesn't care where you are. It just needs diesel and a reason to keep going."

The T5 has 105,000 km on it. It starts every morning. That's all you need to know.
